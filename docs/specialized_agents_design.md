# Specialized Agents: Verification and Obsidian Info Management

This document outlines the design and integration of two new specialized agents within the `vs-brain` framework: the **Verification Agent** and the **Obsidian Agent**.

## 1. Verification and Counter-Arguments Agent

### Objective
To enhance the robustness and objectivity of agent outputs by critically evaluating claims, identifying potential biases, and presenting counter-arguments.

### Key Responsibilities
*   **Claim Verification**: Checking the factual accuracy of statements against reliable sources.
*   **Bias Detection**: Identifying potential biases or one-sided perspectives in generated content.
*   **Counter-Argument Generation**: Providing alternative viewpoints and challenging assumptions to ensure a balanced perspective.
*   **Robustness Review**: Assessing the overall quality and reliability of a specialized agent's output.

### Conditional Activation
The Verification Agent does not activate for every task. Its activation is triggered by the Orchestrator or Assistant based on:
*   **Task Complexity**: High-complexity tasks or those involving sensitive information.
*   **Risk Level**: Tasks with significant potential impact or those requiring high accuracy.
*   **Explicit Request**: When the user or another agent specifically requests a verification step.
*   **Conflicting Information**: When specialized agents provide contradictory or highly uncertain data.

## 2. Obsidian Info Management Agent

### Objective
To provide seamless integration with Obsidian for organizing, storing, and retrieving knowledge gathered and generated by the agent system.

### Key Responsibilities
*   **Knowledge Organization**: Categorizing and structuring information into Obsidian notes and folders.
*   **Note Creation and Updating**: Automatically creating new notes or appending information to existing ones.
*   **Linking and Backlinking**: Establishing connections between related pieces of information within the Obsidian vault.
*   **Knowledge Retrieval**: Searching and retrieving relevant information from the Obsidian vault to inform other agents.

### Integration Tool: Obsidian Connector
The agent utilizes a specialized tool to interact with the Obsidian vault, typically via a local filesystem interface or a dedicated API if available.

## Workflow Integration

Both agents are integrated into the multi-agent orchestration:
1.  **Orchestrator/Assistant** decides if the **Verification Agent** is needed for a specific sub-task result.
2.  The **Obsidian Agent** is invoked at various stages to record findings, organize research, and provide context from the user's existing knowledge base.

## Permission and Risk

*   **Verification Agent**: Primarily "Read-only" and "Draft" actions. Low-risk.
*   **Obsidian Agent**: "External Write" actions (modifying the Obsidian vault). These require careful permission management and potentially human approval for significant changes, following the "draft-commit" pattern.
